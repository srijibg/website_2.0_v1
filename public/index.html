Precision medicine and clinical analytics.

Quantitative pharmacology and machine learning at the point of care.

  • Precision medicine

  • A one-size fits all treatment approach has failed. The FDA estimates that the cost of drug-related morbidity and mortality is $136 billion annually, which is more than the total cost of cardiovascular or diabetic care in the United States. In 2008, ADEs were noted in approximately 1.9 million inpatient hospital stays in the U.S (4.7 percent of all stays), and 840,000 treat-and-release emergency department visits. These ADEs resulted in average hospital stay of 6.7 days and $14,000 of total hospital costs per patient4.

  • InsightRX advantage

  • InsightRX applies quantitative pharmacology and machine learning methods at the point of care in the form of easy to use clinical decision support tools. Quantitative pharmacology translates the relationship(s) between disease, drug action, and individual variability to enable clinical decision making processes to improve patient outcomes.

     

  • Smarter healthcare

  • InsightRX combines machine learning methods with quantitative pharmacology to enhance care at the patient level and population level. At the individual level, biomarker measurements over time are used to optimize treatment strategies. At a population level, as more patient and drug data is collected over time in an infrequently encountered patient population, the system learns and fine-tunes its treatment recommendations.

     

Platform Applications

  • Precision dosing

  • InsightRX leverages peer-reviewed pharmacokinetic models, patient physiology, pharmacogenomics, drug concentrations and biomarkers to optimize dosing. Real-time patient data and machine learning are combined to inform individual patient pharmacology.

  • Disease modeling

  • InsightRX leverages disease-based pharmacodynamic models to inform treatment decision making. Real-time patient data and machine learning are combined to simulate treatment and disease trajectories and inform a patient's physiological state.

     

  • Clinical analytics

  • InsightRX clinical analytics platform functionality ties together treatment strategies with patient outcomes. The data collected as part of platform use will be leveraged to continuosly learn about patient populations, provide patient benchmarking and exposure/response metrics.

     

Our team

Holds a doctorate degree from the department of Bioengineering and Therapeutic Sciences at UCSF, with a research focus in genomics, pharmacokinetics and pharmacometrics. Sirj was a Discovery Fellow at UCSF and has over four years of industry experience at Genentech and Pfizer, working in drug development, spanning departments of biostatistics and clinical pharmacology. His passion lies in bringing genomics and advanced analytics to the forefront of healthcare, which he brings into practice as CEO of InsightRX.

Ron Keizer, Founder

Pharmacist with a PhD in pharmacometrics, Ron has (co-)authored 30+ peer-reviewed publications in pharmacometrics (the application of mathematical-statistical models in drug development or drug treatment). Ron is also a software engineer and the creator of Pirana, an IDE for applied modeling & simulation that is used by 500+ researchers in pharmaceutical industry and academia. After a post-doc at Uppsala University, Ron joined as a senior researcher at UCSF, and now leads the scientific and technological development at InsightRX.

Ranvir Mangat, Founder

A bioengineer by training Ranvir has over 5 years of experience as a pharmacometrics researcher in the Clinical Pharmacology Group at Genentech. Ranvir has led PK/PD analytics for a number of drug candidates in clinical development for early and late stage clinical trials, including global regulatory filings and post-marketing Phase IV studies for the blockbuster HER2 targeted drug franchise. His interests are in applying next generation software applications to leverage real world clinical data and facilitate drug development. Ranvir is our COO at InsightRX.
